FINAL PROJECT

Work in progress

Define a concept and follow that track

Iâ€™ve been thinking about some ideas for a possible final projects, not only about the final project itself but also about a concept to follow like a track throughout all the semester.
Because of my background as a designer with a broad range of interests, I don't want to decide right now one specific field of application of the final project. I'd rather follow a concept-track and see what inspires me most during the classes. This opportunity is also one of the features I love about the Textile Academy Schedule (final project in the last months).

  • Recycling waste materials, reusing of common materials, extraction of raw materials from common find objects. (One of the first thing that impressed me in China is the "overpackaging" of many items, with the inevitable creation of plastic and paper waste)

  • Trying to work on garments/clothing/products from the Chinese tradition, and investigate how a foreigner (an Italian that has a similar thousands-years-long cultural heritage) may interpret it with a "proactive ignorance" and of course with the use of the innovative tools and techniques of the Fablab and Biolab.

    This concept is not limited to clothing, but it may apply also to traditional woodworking. I am pretty excited about the possibilities offered by digital fabrication and soft materials. For example, the same CAD design used to cut wood on the CNC Machine, could be use as a base to design other parts of the same product made with fabric or other soft materials in order to create an integrated system of different digitally fabricated materials.

  • Textile applied to food/kitchen/packaging: the scaffolding class sounds like something that can be applied to a the creation of small structures. Textile materials can be washed and reused or they can be biodegradable or even edible.
